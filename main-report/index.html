Context

Who LifeUSA is

Life for Relief and Development is a humanitarian nonprofit. The website supports trust-building, program discovery, donations, emergency campaigns, and public credibility.

Who Saiaf is

Saiaf Gamal is engaged as LifeUSA's SEO consultant to diagnose organic search problems, prioritize fixes, guide implementation, and report progress clearly to leadership.

What the role covers

Technical SEO, site structure, indexation, metadata, schema, multilingual signals, subdomain consolidation, content optimization guidance, and coordination with the client's web team.

What success looks like

More qualified organic traffic, cleaner search coverage, fewer competing URLs, stronger nonprofit search signals, and a website structure that supports donors instead of fragmenting them.

Where We Started

The starting point was not a lack of effort or authority. The problem was fragmentation: search equity, donor journeys, page signals, and tracking were split across too many surfaces.

Technical baseline

The initial crawl and audit found 54 technical SEO issues, with five major gaps affecting search visibility, site understanding, and donor-path clarity.

Search underperformance

LifeUSA had strong domain authority, but only a small set of pages were meaningfully ranking. Roughly one-third of crawled URLs carried noindex signals, which limited the site’s ability to convert authority into search coverage.

Domain and donor fragmentation

Multiple subdomains and donation surfaces were splitting SEO equity and donor data. This is both a search issue and an operational reporting issue.

Structured data and language signals

The site needed nonprofit-specific schema, better article schema, cleaner multilingual signals, and fixes for language/header inconsistencies.
