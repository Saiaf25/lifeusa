## Article Format

Cautious explanatory guide with practical donor questions.

Do not make this a "10 ways" article. The pillar already owns the broad list. This article owns a narrower decision: whether zakat can be used for orphan sponsorship and what a donor should confirm first.

## Intent

The reader is not asking generally how to help orphans. They are asking:

- Can my zakat sponsor an orphan?
- Does orphan status automatically make a child zakat-eligible?
- What if I want to support orphans but the program is not confirmed as zakat-eligible?
- Is sadaqah safer when eligibility is uncertain?
- What should I ask before donating?

## Safe Answer

Zakat can be used to support orphaned children when the child or household qualifies as zakat-eligible, commonly because they are poor or needy. Orphan status alone does not automatically make someone zakat-eligible, so donors should make sure their zakat is routed through a reviewed zakat-eligible designation.

## Unsafe Claims To Avoid

- Do not say all orphan sponsorships count as zakat.
- Do not say every orphan automatically qualifies for zakat.
- Do not say LifeUSA guarantees this orphan donation is zakat-eligible unless LifeUSA confirms that policy.
- Do not say zakat and sadaqah are interchangeable.
- Do not give a final fiqh ruling. Keep the article donor-facing and cautious.
